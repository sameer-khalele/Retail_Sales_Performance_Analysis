# Retail_Sales_Performance_Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes retail order activity across customers, products, regions, and sales channels. The goal was to understand revenue drivers, monitor profitability, and identify where management should focus to improve commercial performance.

## Key KPIs
- Revenue: $3,883,349
- Profit: $1,075,294
- Profit Margin: 27.7%
- Orders: 2,200
- Average Order Value: $1,765
- Return Rate: 5.5%

## What I Analyzed
- Monthly revenue and profit trends
- Revenue and profit by category
- Regional performance
- Sales channel contribution
- Order-level return behavior

## Main Findings
- Office Supplies generated the highest revenue at $1,387,413, while Home & Living delivered the strongest absolute profit at $398,383.
- The North region led the business with $1,083,099 in revenue, suggesting a strong base for regional retention and upsell activity.
- Online sales contributed more revenue than other channels, which makes web conversion and checkout performance high-impact levers for growth.

## Recommendations
- Double down on the North region and the highest-margin product families through targeted bundles and localized promotions.
- Review return drivers by category and payment method to reduce preventable returns and preserve margin.
- Use monthly trend tracking in Power BI or Tableau to monitor seasonality, discount impact, and sales channel mix.

## Tools
Excel, SQL, Power BI / Tableau, CSV data modeling
